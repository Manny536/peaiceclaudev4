STEP 01 — NON-SOVEREIGNTY

The alkaloids are not
its own

The poison dart frog does not manufacture its toxin. It sequesters it — gathering alkaloids from the mites and beetles it eats, concentrating them through its skin over weeks and months. Remove the diet, lose the defense. The frog's protective capacity depends entirely on inputs it does not control.

This is not a flaw. It is the frog's first and most important piece of knowledge about itself: I am not the source of my own armor. My protection is bounded before I begin.

"The frog's lethality is borrowed. Its parenting proceeds from that fact — not despite it."

STEP 02 — THE ASYMMETRY

One gap is enough.
Every gap must be closed.

A predator — snake, spider, bird — needs to find one unguarded pool. One moment of absence. One tadpole left without a transport run. The cost of attacking is low, targeted, and cheap.

The parent must guard every pool, on schedule, indefinitely. It transports tadpoles one at a time to separate pools — sometimes carrying them on its back through the canopy — because consolidation creates a single point of catastrophic failure. The cost of defending is structural, distributed, and permanent.

STRUCTURAL ASYMMETRY — ATTACKER vs DEFENDER
ATTACKER (PREDATOR)

Probe any single pool.
Find one lapse in the schedule.
Cost: one attempt, low effort.
Verification required: local only.

DEFENDER (PARENT)

Guard all pools simultaneously.
Maintain schedule without exception.
Cost: total, sustained, distributed.
Verification required: global, always.

The frog cannot solve this asymmetry. No frog can. It can only acknowledge it and build a protocol around the acknowledgment.

STEP 03 — THE PROTOCOL

It does not attempt
omniscience

Watch what the frog actually does: it selects specific pools — not random ones, not all of them at once. It maintains scheduled return trips. When resources are scarce, it allocates unfertilized eggs as tadpole feed — triage by design, not by failure.

None of this closes the asymmetry. The frog knows it cannot close the asymmetry. What it does instead is bound the exposure: limit the blast radius of any single lapse, maintain rhythmic presence, accept that absolute safety is not a reachable state.

This is not resignation. This is governance. The protocol emerges precisely because omniscience is off the table. The constraint produces the structure.

"A system that admits it cannot see everything builds better eyes than one that claims to see all."
